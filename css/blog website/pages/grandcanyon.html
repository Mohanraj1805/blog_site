Grand Canyon Travel Guide

Last Updated: August 2, 2023

The Grand Canyon is one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World and is located in the northern part of Arizona. Known for its breathtaking vistas and geological significance, the Grand Canyon attracts millions of visitors each year. Whether you’re hiking the canyon's trails, rafting the Colorado River, or simply taking in the scenic views from the various lookout points, the Grand Canyon offers an unforgettable experience. The area around the Grand Canyon is rustic, with limited access to modern amenities, but it is rich in natural beauty.

Budget Hotel Prices – Budget accommodations near the Grand Canyon typically start at $80-120 per night. These can be basic motels or lodges that offer amenities like private bathrooms, free WiFi, and air-conditioning. Alternatively, campgrounds around the Grand Canyon are also a popular and cheaper option, with fees starting around $20-30 per night for tent camping.

Average Cost of Food – Food at the Grand Canyon can be a bit more expensive due to its remote location. Budget meals, such as those at cafes or fast food joints, will typically cost between $8-15 per meal. For a more substantial meal at sit-down restaurants, expect to pay $20-30 per person. If you plan on cooking, groceries for a week can cost around $50-70, which can include basics like pasta, vegetables, and snacks for your trip.

Transportation Costs – The Grand Canyon is a popular tourist destination, but it is located in a relatively remote area. Visitors usually need to rent a car to reach the park, with rental rates starting at $30-50 per day. Once at the Grand Canyon, visitors can use the free shuttle buses that circulate around the park. A ride on these shuttles is included in the park entry fee, which is $35 for a vehicle. If you prefer a more adventurous option, you can take guided tours of the park via jeep or helicopter, with prices ranging from $50 for a basic tour to $250 for a helicopter ride.
