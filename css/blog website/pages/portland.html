Portland Travel Guide

Last Updated: August 2, 2024

Portland, known as the "City of Roses," is a vibrant and eclectic city in the Pacific Northwest of the United States. Famous for its beautiful natural surroundings, including lush green parks and proximity to mountains and the coast, Portland offers an outdoor lover's paradise. The city is known for its laid-back vibe, unique neighborhoods, and growing food scene, especially the food truck culture. Portland’s residents take pride in their environmentally conscious lifestyle and the city has an active and welcoming community. While Portland may not have the hustle of larger cities, it’s rich in character and creativity. It's easy to get around the city via public transport, bike, or by walking.

Budget Hotel Prices – Budget hotels in Portland typically start around $70-90 per night. Basic amenities such as free WiFi, private bathrooms, and air-conditioning are common. For a more local experience, you can find Airbnb options with shared rooms starting at $40-50 per night, and entire apartments starting from $100 per night.

Average Cost of Food – Portland is known for its diverse food scene, and it caters to all budgets. You can enjoy food trucks offering tacos, sandwiches, and vegan meals starting at $5-10. Sit-down meals at mid-range restaurants typically range between $12-25 per person. For groceries, expect to pay around $50-70 per week for essentials like vegetables, pasta, and chicken. Farmers markets and local produce stands are popular for fresh and affordable food options.

Transportation Costs – Portland is well-connected by public transportation, including buses, the MAX Light Rail, and streetcars. A single ride on any of these costs $2.50, and a day pass for unlimited rides is available for $5. For a more eco-friendly experience, Portland has an excellent biking culture, and bike rentals cost around $10-20 per day. Uber and Lyft are also affordable options within the city, with rides typically costing between $7-15 depending on distance.
